Transfr Inc

At Transfr, as a Senior XR Designer, I helped shape the future of learning by building immersive Virtual Reality simulations that empower people to explore careers, gain practical skills, and unlock new opportunities. My work cut across XR interaction design, systems engineering, and AI-driven workflow automation—always with the goal of making VR education scalable, efficient, and impactful.

I contributed to two distinct product lines at Transfr:

  1. Hands-on Training Simulations – realistic, interactive modules where learners practice job-ready skills in safe, virtual environments.
  2. Career Exploration Simulations – immersive experiences that let learners "step into" different professions, understand roles, and discover pathways to meaningful work.

Here's a few sims/products I have worked on:

Prototyping and Feature Improvement of Transfr SDK

Transfr's innovation depended on tools that could keep pace with ambitious ideas. I played a key role in designing, testing, and prototyping with our proprietary SDK tools, which powered the development of new simulations.

One of my most impactful contributions was work closely with engineering team to develop and improve a node-based editor for Unity simulation development—a visual scripting environment that dramatically streamlined production. This editor empowered both designers and developers to build complex interactions quickly, while maintaining consistency and scalability across the product ecosystem.

Introducing Modular Interaction Frameworks & Templates

To keep simulations consistent, modular, and instructional by design, I created frameworks and templates that embedded instructional UX patterns into every experience.

These systems include interactable assets (like drill, hammer, screwdriver, etc.), UI templates, and other reusable components that can be used to build simulations:

  • Standardized XR interactions across dozens of simulations.
  • Ensured modules could be reused and reconfigured for different industries and training contexts.
  • Reduced production friction, allowing studios and designers to build faster without reinventing the wheel.

In short, I helped turn VR training from a collection of one-off projects into a scalable simulation ecosystem.

Automating with AI Workflows

Beyond simulation design, I also built AI-powered automation pipelines that accelerated production and unlocked new capabilities. Using tools like n8n, I developed workflows for:

  1. Asset retrieval – quickly sourcing and categorizing 3D assets.
  2. Script generation – converting raw instructional content into coach-ready dialogue.
  3. Document conversion & data handling – ensuring knowledge could flow smoothly across formats.
  4. Agentic bots – intelligent assistants that reduced manual overhead for production teams.

These AI workflows turned repetitive bottlenecks into automated processes, freeing teams to focus on creativity and innovation.
